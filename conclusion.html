Financial Learning Conclusions

Our unsupervised learning models demonstrated that there were four important classes, one of which was dominant, containing the majority of companies. We also demonstrated that most companies stayed within a single class over a ten year period but there were some companies that switched classes from year to year. Apple, for example, was a member of eight different classes over the past decade.

When we applied a singular value decomposition, we found that component number two correlated very strongly with class, and that singular value decomposition was useful when applied to raw data.

Our supervised learning models involved two trained classifiers and produced consistent results in the top features that impacted the current year’s market capitalization.

The regression analysis ran its own classifier to identify the top 10-k variables affecting next year’s market capitalization. Using both the Supervised Learning features and a Random Forest classifier for next year’s market cap relations, we discovered that the model using non-normalized data correctly identified 56% of companies as increasing or decreasing in market capitalization.

Our overall conclusion was that machine learning, in combination with regression analysis, could be a very valuable source of information to investors.
